Reference Sequence

In terms of Genetics, Reference Sequence is a set of genetic instructions encoded in four deoxyribonucleotides 'A (adenine), T (Thymine), G (Guanine), C (Cytosine)' also called bases. The bases are held together by 'hydrogen bonds', and are arranged in sequence to make a DNA strand. Different sections of DNA strand carry instructions that are used by the cells to make macromolecules that are essential for all known forms of life. There are few publically available reference sequence databases that are annotated and curated by experts and can be used in Genetic Data Analysis.

Why Reference Sequences are important?

It is essential to use common standards as references throughout the scientific research community to report reproducible discoveries. Reference Sequences are standards for studying genetics in all forms of life. The genetic sequences generated in scientific experiments are compared against Reference Sequences of the organisms to study functions of genes and/or find anomalies that cause genetic diseases, and find ways to treat diseases caused by such anomalies.
